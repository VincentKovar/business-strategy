AI Role Audit Guide

A Jobs-to-Be-Done approach to mapping your role against AI capabilities.
Read more about the JTBD Framework →

Why use the Jobs-to-Be-Done framework?

Instead of asking: "What can AI do?" — let's ask a better question: "What are you actually trying to get done — and where is the friction that's costing you time and energy?"

That shift in framing is what makes this exercise produce real actions and save you from having to learn 100 tools.

The Jobs-to-Be-Done Framework
"People don't buy products. They hire them to get a job done."
— Clayton Christensen, Harvard Business School

Originally developed as a product strategy framework, JTBD reframes how we think about value. Instead of asking what a tool does, it asks what outcome a person is trying to achieve — and what is standing in the way.

Applied to your career, JTBD helps you see your role not as a list of tasks, but as a set of jobs you are hired to accomplish. Each job has:

Functional job The concrete outcome you are responsible for delivering — the 'what'
Micro-jobs The smaller, repeatable tasks that make up how you deliver that outcome — the 'how'
Friction points The parts of those micro-jobs that are slow, frustrating, repetitive, or low-value — the 'where it hurts'
Friction is where AI creates the most immediate value. This audit is designed to find it.
Step 1

Map Your Jobs & Friction Points

Identify what you are hired to do — and where it costs you the most

Part A · Your Core Functional Job

Your core functional job is the single most important outcome your role exists to deliver. Not your job title — the result your organisation would notice if it stopped happening.

Example

Job title: Operations Manager

Core functional job: Ensure the business runs predictably so leadership can focus on growth

Part B · Your Micro-Jobs & Friction Points

List the recurring tasks that make up how you deliver your core job. Then rate each one: how much friction does it create? (1 = low, 3 = high). Be honest — the high-friction items are your biggest AI opportunities.

Micro-Job (recurring task) Friction Point (what makes it hard / slow) Score 1–3

Circle or highlight your highest-friction items before moving to Step 2.

Step 2

AI Integration Strategy

For each micro-job, choose the right relationship with AI

Not every task belongs in the same AI relationship. The three archetypes below define how much human judgment stays in the loop — and choosing the right one is what separates effective AI use from frustrating AI use.

⚙

Automate

Hands-off execution

AI handles the task completely based on predefined rules or inputs. You review the output — you do not co-create it.

Best for tasks that are:
  • Repetitive and rule-based
  • Low-risk if slightly imperfect
  • Time-consuming but low-judgment
Example micro-job: Summarising meeting notes into action items
Try this prompt:
"Here are my meeting notes. Extract all action items, assign owners where mentioned, and format as a table with columns: Action, Owner, Deadline."
⚡

Augment

AI does the heavy lifting — you apply the judgment

AI handles the analysis, synthesis, or first draft. You act as the editor and strategic filter — shaping, refining, and approving the output.

Best for tasks that are:
  • Complex but pattern-based
  • High output volume, moderate stakes
  • Expertise-dependent with known structure
Example micro-job: Writing a project status report for leadership
Try this prompt:
"Here is my raw project data and notes. Draft a 1-page leadership update: headline status, 3 key updates, top risk, and recommended next step. Tone: direct and confident."
🤝

Partner

Interactive thinking — you and AI building ideas together

Interactive brainstorming, scenario testing, or stress-testing ideas. You bounce concepts back and forth — AI challenges your thinking, fills gaps, and surfaces angles you haven't considered.

Best for tasks that are:
  • High-stakes decisions or strategies
  • Novel problems without clear answers
  • Ideas that need challenging before sharing
Example micro-job: Preparing for a difficult stakeholder conversation
Try this prompt:
"I have a tough conversation coming up with [stakeholder]. Their concern is X. My position is Y. Play devil's advocate — what are the three strongest arguments against my position, and how should I respond to each?"
Now map your micro-jobs to an archetype: Go back to your list from Step 1. Assign each one: Automate (A), Augment (AU), or Partner (P). Focus on your highest-friction items first.

Step 3

Your AI Action Plan

Select your High-ROI Quick Win and build your first Claude habit

What is a High-ROI Quick Win?

A High-ROI Quick Win is a micro-job that is:

  • Repetitive — you do it at least weekly
  • Low-risk — an imperfect first output is recoverable
  • High friction — it currently costs you meaningful time or energy

Examples: meeting synthesis, data formatting, initial drafting, email summarising, report structuring

Look at your mapping table from Step 2. Your Quick Win is almost always the task with a friction score of 3 that you assigned to Automate or Augment — because those are the places where AI can take the most work off your plate immediately.

Remember

This audit is not a one-time exercise. Revisit it every 90 days — or whenever your role changes. As AI capabilities grow, tasks you could only Partner on today may become Automate candidates tomorrow.
