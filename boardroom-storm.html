Boardroom STORM

Decision-grade research

Adapted from Stanford OVAL Lab's STORM method. Built for Claude.
Stage 1 of 6

Frame your question

STORM starts before writing — with a decision-grade question. A good question names a decision-maker, a decision point, and a consequence if the call goes wrong. Vague topics produce vague research.

Stage 2 of 6

Generate ten perspectives

STORM's edge comes from forcing ten decision-relevant expert lenses before any writing begins. Each perspective must have real skin in the game — genuine incentive to get this question right or wrong.

Operator insiderSomeone currently doing this in the market
Failed entrantSomeone who tried and pulled back
Bear analystThe most credible pessimist

Regulator / policyRules, moats, and friction
End customerThe buyer's actual experience
Capital allocatorWhere the money actually flows
Technical constraintWhat the tech makes hard or easy
Cultural brokerWhat gets lost in translation

Stage 3 of 6

Run source-grounded interviews

For each perspective, Claude adopts the expert role and answers with real sources — not generic opinions. Run one perspective at a time, or ask Claude to batch all ten. Every unsourced claim gets flagged explicitly.

Tip: Run the highest-stakes or most contrarian perspectives first. The bear and the failed entrant surface problems the bull will rationalize away.

Stage 4 of 6

Build the contradiction ledger

Track where expert perspectives flatly disagree. Contradictions are your most valuable signals — the places where easy consensus breaks down. Log each one, then ask Claude to resolve or explain it without splitting the difference.

Stage 5 of 6

Synthesize a source-mapped outline

STORM's core rule: draft only from a stable outline. Every section must trace back to at least one source from the interview stage. No unsourced claim reaches the draft.

What makes a good STORM outline: Organized by decision relevance, not topic category. Contradictions that weren't resolved get their own section. Each entry carries its source and open questions.

Stage 6 of 6

Draft, then run the moderator audit

Claude drafts only from the approved outline. Then a Co-STORM moderator pass hunts for unknown unknowns — the gaps most reports never surface. Run both prompts in sequence.
